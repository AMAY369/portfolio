### **Other Services (Secondary/Complementary)**
These support your core offerings and are positioned as add-ons or complementary services:

5. **Digital Marketing**
   - Complements development projects
   - Not a standalone primary service
   - Positioned as "complete digital solution" when combined with development

6. **Technical Consulting**
   - Advisory and architecture services
   - CTO-as-a-Service for non-technical founders
   - Project-based or retainer engagements

---

## Rationale for Change

### Why Move Digital Marketing to Secondary?

**1. Competitive Positioning:**
- Most freelance developers don't offer marketing at all
- Positioning it as complementary makes it a bonus, not a distraction
- Keeps focus on your core technical expertise

**2. Market Perception:**
- Clients looking for developers want development first
- Too many services can dilute your positioning
- "Jack of all trades, master of none" concern

**3. Service Hierarchy:**
- Development services are higher value ($15K-40K projects)
- Marketing services typically lower engagement value
- Better as upsell or package add-on

**4. Portfolio Focus:**
- Case studies will primarily showcase development work
- Marketing is supportive, not primary skill
- Easier to establish authority in 4 core areas than 6 equal areas

## Questions & Considerations

### FAQ:

**Q: Won't I lose marketing clients?**
A: Unlikely. Clients needing marketing + development will find you. Marketing-only clients probably weren't your target anyway (different price point, skillset focus).

**Q: Should I remove marketing entirely?**
A: No! Keep it as secondary. It's a competitive advantage to offer "complete digital solutions" when combined with development.

**Q: What if I want to pivot to marketing focus later?**
A: Easy to adjust. For now, establish development authority first, then can expand marketing prominence if desired.

**Q: How do I position consulting?**
A: "Technical consulting" or "CTO advisory" positioned for non-technical founders who need strategic guidance alongside (or before) development.
